Review the synthetic hard-case stress-suite unit on branch

The unit's KEY CRITERION: where data cannot distinguish models the engine
must REPORT ambiguity, never confidently pick one; where there IS a right
answer it must recover it. Review for:

1. Ground-truth honesty: are the generators actually producing what they
   claim (Poisson noise so 1/sqrt weights are correct by construction; the
   documented χ²ᵣ floor from Lorentzian tails under the endpoint-anchored
   linear background — is the floor explanation correct and honestly
   labeled a design property rather than hidden)?
2. Classification-rule soundness (summarize_stress_battery.py): do the
   rules force-pass anything? Is "confident_true(RELABEL?)" on
   ambiguous-class cases honest (evidence the case was mislabeled) rather
   than a laundering of overconfidence? Any hole where a method could
   overclaim structure and still be classified favorably?
3. Scaffold-vs-engine attribution: the distinct-window ladder exists
   because identical windows label-switch and zero persistence (documented
   in _n_peak_ladder). Is anything in the scaffold biasing FOR the engine
   (windows that pre-encode the answer beyond what a real region grammar
   would know)?
4. The always-on pins (test_stress_honesty.py): do they pin engine
   behavior that is genuinely invariant-worthy, or measured happenstance
   that will flake (seeded, so deterministic — but are the tolerances
   principled)?
5. Findings supported by the JSONL: n_refits=4 basin failures at high S/N;
   Bayesian small-budget P3 overfit on a recover case; sparse over-
   splitting on sub-FWHM overlap; the LS sub-FWHM position drift. Any
   misreading of the evidence?

Findings with file:line, severity, failure scenario, fix. Do not modify
anything. End with exactly 'VERDICT: GO' or 'VERDICT: NO-GO (blockers: ...)'.
